CW-Node Research Roadmap

From Experimental Lab to Published Research

Phase 1: Foundation (Current)

1. The 10M Parameter Proof of Concept

Objective: Prove fundamental theoretical viability.
  • Successfully complete the 3-day marathon on the 200M token dataset.
  • Prove definitively that both the 30/70 and 70/30 CW-Node splits achieve a lower validation loss than the mathematically equivalent Dense Baseline at 10M parameters.
Success Condition: The final validation loss curve of the CW-Node stays consistently below the Dense model after 1 full epoch.
Phase 2: Upgrade

2. Tokenization & Context Upgrade

Objective: Move from "toy model" to "real language model".
  • Discard the character-level tokenizer (vocab size 65).
  • Implement a standard Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) subword tokenizer (vocab size ~32k - 50k) to drastically improve token efficiency and context window scope.
Success Condition: The model generates coherent English words and philosophical ideas in significantly fewer training steps without needing to learn how to spell.
Phase 3: Validation

3. Scaling Law Validation (50M - 100M)

Objective: Prove the architecture doesn't plateau at small scales.
  • Many novel architectures look amazing at 10M parameters but fail when scaled up.
  • Train a 50M to 100M parameter CW-Node model on a larger dataset (e.g., 2B - 5B tokens).
  • Accept that training will be slower than a Dense network in PyTorch due to the custom routing, but focus entirely on the parameter efficiency and loss curve.
Success Condition: The validation loss scaling curve remains strictly superior to a 100M parameter Dense Transformer (like GPT-2 Small).
Phase 4: Publication

4. Research Publication & Open Source

Objective: Share the mathematical breakthrough with the scientific community.
  • Acknowledge that competing with tech giants on bare-metal CUDA optimization is not the goal of a solo researcher.
  • Write a formal research paper detailing the mathematical foundation of the CW-Node and the empirical proof that it achieves lower loss per parameter than Dense networks.
  • Open-source the PyTorch codebase and checkpoints.
Success Condition: Present the CW-Node to the open-source AI community (arXiv, GitHub), allowing engineers worldwide to take your mathematical blueprint and write the optimized C++/CUDA kernels for it.
